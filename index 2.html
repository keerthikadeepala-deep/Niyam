THE PROBLEM

The platform is validated. Your configuration of it is your problem.

Vendors validate their platform. Regulators hold you responsible for your configured, integrated, in-use system — and every vendor release changes it underneath you.

3× a year

Salesforce pushes major releases on a fixed train. They deploy whether your revalidation is finished or not — the timeline is theirs, the accountability is yours.

Weeks per cycle

Each release means reading hundreds of pages of release notes, assessing impact on your workflows, executing regression tests, and writing documentation your quality unit will sign.

One audit question

"Show me your assessment of the last platform release." A thin answer turns a routine audit into a data-integrity conversation nobody wants.

EVERY RELEASE CYCLE, YOU RECEIVE

A complete, audit-ready validation package — not a tool to operate.

Most release cycles end with "no validation needed" — but an auditor still expects the documented assessment that proves it. We deliver that proof every cycle, and the full validation package on the cycles that genuinely need one. Judgment plus finished work, not a tool to operate.

EVERY CYCLE

Release impact assessment

Every vendor release note analyzed against your actual configuration — auto-enabled changes, retirements, integrations, custom code — with impacted functions identified and risk-rated. Not a generic summary: yours.

EVERY CYCLE

Documented no-impact rationale

When nothing warrants testing, you receive the signed assessment saying so and why — the answer to "show me your evaluation of the last platform release" that keeps a routine audit routine.

WHEN TRIGGERED

Risk-based regression & evidence

When an assessment flags real impact — or you bundle feature adoption and enhancements into a planned change — we execute a CSA-aligned targeted regression in your sandbox, with results documented and deviations dispositioned.

WHEN TRIGGERED

Validation summary package

The signed, versioned package your quality unit files within your own change control: assessment, strategy, evidence, conclusion — ready before go-live, inside your SDLC, under your signatures.

HOW AN ENGAGEMENT RUNS

Set up once. Covered every release after.

STEP 1 · WEEK 1–2

Baseline

We inventory your configuration, integrations, and critical quality workflows, and agree the risk framework with your quality unit.

STEP 2 · WEEK 3–4

Test suite

We build (or adopt) your regression suite, mapped to requirements and risk — maintained by us from then on.

STEP 3 · EVERY RELEASE

Assess & decide

Preview window opens; we assess and deliver the impact verdict. No impact: rationale filed, done. Impact: targeted validation executes before go-live.

STEP 4 · ALWAYS

Stand behind it

Auditor asks about release management? The package is in your files, and we'll support you in the room if needed.
